John Lennon

John Lennon, born in October 9, 1940, Liverpool, England, was the leader/co-leader of The Beatles, author, graphic artist , solo recording artist and collaborator with Yoko Ono, who he married at the age of 28. At age 16, he founded a band that evolved into The Beatles.

Paul McCartney

Paul McCartney, born in June 18, 1942, Liverpool, England, was a vocalist, songwriter, composer, bass player, poet and painter. He was one of the most popular solo performers of all time in terms of both sales of his recordings and attendance of concerts.

George Harrison

George Harrison, born in February 25, 1943, Liverpool, England, was a musician, singer, songwriter and guitarist of The Beatles. He was the youngest and later achieved singular success as a songwriter and performer.

Ringo Starr

Ringo Starr, born in July 7, 1940, Liverpool, England, was a musician, singer, songwriter, actor and drummer of The Beatles. He was introduced to the drums by a health worker who gave children musical instruments to amuse them when he got pleurisy at the age of 13. He then found success in a solo career.
